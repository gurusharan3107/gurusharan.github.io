The Seed and the Stars

A fable about perspective, growth, and finding what you seek

Part One

The Dream Beneath the Earth

Once there was a seed buried deep in the darkness of the soil. While others slept, this seed dreamed of stars—those distant points of light it had heard whispered of in the wind's passing tales.

"I must reach them," the seed would whisper to itself. "I must touch the stars, no matter how far they may be."

02

Part Two

The Rising

Seasons turned. The seed broke through its shell, sending roots downward and a shoot upward—always upward, toward the unseen light it had imagined for so long.

Through rain and storm, through wind and drought, the seedling grew. It became a sapling, then a tree. And still it stretched skyward, certain the stars were just beyond its reach.

03

Part Three

The Reflection

One evening, after years of reaching, the tree grew weary. The stars still seemed impossibly far. It lowered its branches in resignation, tears of dew falling onto the leaves below.

And then it saw them. There, in a single drop of dew—reflected tiny stars, dancing in the light of the very sky it had sought. The stars had been with it all along, reflected in what it had become.

The Stars Within
